High-latitude coronal mass ejections on the young solar-like star AB Dor

K. M. Strickert, D. Evensberget, A. A. Vidotto

Monthly Notices of the Royal Astronomical Society 2024 Vol. 533, pp. 1156–1165

This work investigates coronal mass ejections (CMEs) on AB Dor, a young solar-like star with a surface magnetic field 100-1000 times stronger than the Sun's. Using 3D magnetohydrodynamic simulations, I modeled 21 CME scenarios at high latitudes to understand how strong magnetic fields constrain eruptions.

Key findings:

  • CMEs in open magnetic field regions are significantly more likely to erupt
  • CMEs in closed magnetic field regions require high free magnetic energies (≥ 3×10³⁵ erg) to escape
  • Magnetic tension limits kinetic energy in confined regions
  • High-latitude eruptive CMEs likely occur in regions of open magnetic field, consistent with AB Dor's observed polar active regions

Galactic Dynamics and the spread of Galactic Civilisations

Markus Strickert

Lund University, Degree Project 2020 15 higher education credits

This bachelor's thesis simulates the potential spread of civilisations through a galaxy via close encounters between star systems. The work combines galactic dynamics (using epicycle approximation) with stochastic models for planetary evolution and civilisation development.

Key findings:

  • Modeled 21 CME scenarios with varying parameters across open and closed magnetic field regions
  • Simulated how probability distributions for life development affect colonization patterns
  • First technologically developed civilisation emerged between 270 million and 4.8 billion years, depending on evolution parameters
  • Colonization followed exponential growth before leveling off as available planets saturated
  • Demonstrated how star density and probability distributions influence competitive dynamics between spreading and stationary civilisations
